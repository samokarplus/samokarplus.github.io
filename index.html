Reflections

Swami warned his male devotees about women and gold. Women and gold. I wonder what would happen if you chopped your dick off. Would that make life far easier? Or far more pointless (literally)? As the gospel of Sri Ramakrishna would say "And this is maya." LOL.

Sometimes, its hard to believe this world is just a dream, nothing more than an illusion. But then in those moments where you really feel only God there, no you, no other, no world left to dream up, it all is so quiet. Paramananda (supreme bliss). I remember reading these words im about to insert for the first time and it brought me to tears haha.

"There is no need of a way out! Don't you see that a way out is also part of the dream. All you have to do is see the dream as dream. ...Wherever it leads you, it will be a dream. The very idea of going beyond the dream is illusory. Why go anywhere Just realize that you are dreaming a dream you call the world and stop looking for ways out. The dream is not your problem. Your problem is that you like one part of your dream and not another. Love all, or none of it, and stop complaining. When you have seen the dream as a dream, you have done all that needs be done."

~Nisargadatta

But that last sentence is true... and untrue. Ultimately yes you have. But practicallly, NO. That's just the start. Then you have to play pretend and pretend to be. a body-mind-heart for so many decades. But I guess this is where Swami Sarvapriyananda's fun little creation story comes in. I find this story so beautiful and the way he presents it is so funny and intelligent. In the beginning there was god. From eternity to eternity sat there. but god got bored. lol. so God decided to play. But who will God play with? There's no one else to play with. So God thought for a long time and made a clever solution. God pretended to be Not God. So now there are two to play with. But there became a problem because God is really good at what god does. so when he was playing god really forgot god was not god. And this not god is searching for who he is. and this is the game of life. LOL i love that story so much.

Is life about playing pretend? Who's playing? Hahaha. The fun part of life is you can discover it's just a dream and merely a game of pretend. But then you can't just escape. You can't just shoot yourself. You (The Divine) have to go back and go live out this beautiful rollercoaster full of dreams, attachments, pain, sensory pleasures. And if it is just a dream, then you can take as much risk and fuck around as much as you want because it's all an illusion.

What's funny is that Advaita Vedanta and other nondual philosophies are heavily focused on the love and perfection of God. But in practical living, it's probably quite a bit closer to optimistic nihilism (ON) than dogmatic religious living. The mentality of ON like "Fuck it. We gonna die someday so might as well live like crazy and do everything." And the approach of Advaita Vedanta is "This world is merely a dream. I am not the doer. But i feel like the doer so I might as well feel and do the best I can."

I also find that people that believe in free will and believe that the world is more than just a dream, it can be advntageous because they can be more driven, often out of fear of not being able to just sit by themselves and sit with their thoughts. Idk. Next time I should write about the intriguing idea of necessary duality and dreams. Do we need the hot for the cold? The pain for the pleasure? The highs for the lows? What functions do these serve if it's all maya? Idk. Probably a swami could tell me that. Lol. Good night.
